Child Poverty, Education and Crime in New Orleans.

How cild poverty and education impact crime in the Crescent City.

By Random Human | Dec. 11, 2018

In 2017, New Orleans, LA was ranked the fourth deadliest city in the United States. Children that grow up in poverty are more likely to face health problems, a higher risk of teen pregnancy, poorer access to a good education, more family life disruptions and a greater propensity to crime according to US Surgeon Generalâ€™s Study on Youth Violence I decided to take a look at if education and poverty levels of children in New Orleans affect local crimes such as homicide, rape, burglary, battery and theft.
